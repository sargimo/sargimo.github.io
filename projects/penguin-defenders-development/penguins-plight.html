The
Plight
of the
Penguin

Over the last 30 years, we have seen the number of mainland penguins on the West Coast plummet. There were once estimated to be between 2 and 3 thousand of our flappy friends, and today that number is estimated to be closer to 2 to 3 hundred. What is causing it? As it turns out, a lot of things.

Many believed originally that the main cause of the decline was due to a low breeding success rate. Studies showed this to not be the case, and that more research was needed to quantify the real problems facing the penguin.

Penguins are generally very busy breeders who have up to two chicks a year. However, penguins require both parents to rear their chicks, meaning that any adult penguin death actually can remove 3 penguins from the population.

As you can imagine, most of the causes of these deaths are caused by humans, and that is why it is our job to educate, research and find ways to restore the safety of our penguin population.

A large number of these deaths came from road deaths. Not all of these can be helped, but educating locals, as well as providing fencing in heavy breeding areas can go a long way to helping.

Other causes of death include predators, with dogs being a big threat, loss of habitat from erosion, development of housing and subdivisions. An alarming number of penguins also die at sea in fishing nets.

More research is urgently needed to help identify and change these patterns. A large amount of our donation money goes to funding research and education, and we appreciate every cent given.
